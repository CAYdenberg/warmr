Predicting the future of climate change

A curious feature of some climate models is that they predict a linear relationship between cumulative carbon emissions and total warming. This means we can try to guess the climate response by extrapolating emissions trends.

Notes:

  1. Emissions data from BP Statistical Review of World Energy
  2. The climate-carbon response (CCR) is estimated in Matthews et al (2009) to be about 1 degree celcius per 1 trillion tonnes carbon, or ~ 0.4 deg celcius per 1 trillion tonnes CO2. The 95% confidence interval for this estimate ranges from 0.2 - 0.6 degrees C, and these values are used to generate the endpoints of the red gradient.
  3. This only takes into account global warming due to CO2, and not other greenhouse gasses.
