Predicting the future of climate change

A curious feature of some climate models is that they predict a linear relationship between cumulative carbon emissions and total warming. This means we can try to guess the climate response by extrapolating emissions trends.

Notes:

  1. Emissions data from BP Statistical Review of World Energy
  2. The climate-carbon response (CCR) is estimated in Matthews et al (2009) to be about 1°C per 1 trillion tonnes carbon, or ~ 0.4°C per 1 trillion tonnes CO₂. The 95% confidence interval for this estimate ranges from 0.2 - 0.6°C, and these values are used to generate the endpoints of the red gradient.
  3. This only takes into account global warming due to CO₂, and not other greenhouse gasses.
  4. This tool reports expected warming compared to 1965 levels. The UN International Panel on Climate Change (IPCC) usually discusses total warming relative to pre-industrial levels.
